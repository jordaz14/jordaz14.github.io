Sort of. My doctor cleared me to walk. But walking is hard.

I'm talking as if my foot situation is now all over, but I'm still in my boot for the next few days as I continue to try putting weight on my foot. Regardless, having to use only one leg for the past two months was actually one of the worst experiences I've gone through in recent memory. If I had to choose between experiencing the pain I felt when first breaking my foot or the following 8 monotonous weeks, I'd happily break my foot again & again & again & again.

Prior to breaking my foot, I never wished for a broken bone, but I always had a curiosity about what it'd be like (and what bone I'd break). Retrospectively, this curiosity was misguided; regardless of what the quote above may suggest, there is no positive way to spin breaking your foot.

However, I will admit that there are probably way better bones to break and still live your life as normal - a broken foot is not one of them. Additionally, I'm a bit afraid of breaking bones going forward. Not because of the pain or the surgeries, but because of the amount of time in my life I'd lose out on; breaking my foot felt like a 2 month pause from my life, and it's still really frustrating to think about the time lost.

Anyway, I'm still not very mobile. Honestly, I'm quicker on crutches right now. But, progress is being made - what a fucking relief.

OSLO, AUGUST 31st by Joachim Trier

Anders : It will get better. Everything will be alright.
[laughing]
Anders : Except it won't, you know.

Ok, I would not recommend watching this movie, only because it is fucking tragic to watch unfold, but maybe that's on me for watching the movie late at night.

Oslo, August 31st is the 2nd movie in Trier's Oslo Trilogy. The 1st being Reprised and the 3rd being The Worst Person in the World. Interestingly, all feature Anders Danielsen Lie as a lead role across the series' 18 years, uniquely capturing him and his characters at different points in their life. He doesn't play the same character across the series, but his age is often a talking point in each of the movies I've seen so far. Lastly, he's an incredible actor with no formal training, and his primary profession is as a doctor in Oslo in-between movies. Incredible.

I'm not trying to compare myself to an Academy-award nominated director, but if I was to make a movie, it'd be in the style of Joachim Trier - across the two movies I've seen, there's a a very raw portrayal of the human experience, depicting flawed protagonists undergoing existential problems in their urban lifestyles. Throw in a couple of literary quotes as well and that's essentially my style of movie.

For context & spoilers, Oslo, August 31st captures the full day of a recovering drug addict, Anders, leaving rehab to visit his family and friends in Oslo. Throughout this day, you slowly watch Anders life fall apart as he struggles to come to terms with the life that awaits him outside of rehab, having spent the past 6 years using and dealing Heroin. My favorite scene from the movie is 'the cafe scene', where Anders sits in a cafe and hears the droning of conversations and life around him. I love this scene because it's the best depiction of loneliness that I've ever seen in media. I'd highly recommend watching this scene on YouTube.
