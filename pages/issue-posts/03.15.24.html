I'd like to Play Alone, Please by Tom Segura

Even if you try to call him Julian, he gets very upset. "I'm not Julian!"

I'm going to do myself in right now. I used to listen to the Joe Rogan podcast a lot. This was pre-pandemic Joe where I feel like things were relatively more tame and he hadn't become a figurehead for anti-covid rhetoric and other conspiracies. He was and still is big on DMT though, and that's pretty cool. Point being, I've been listening to more Joe Rogan podcasts again, particularly the ones with comedians since those are the most fun and relatively harmless, so I listened to the one featuring Tom Segura. Quick aside, the episode with Aaron Rodgers is actually insane. That man is aggressively a conspiracy theorist who likes to "do his own research".

Anyway, Tom Segura is comedian I've liked, and I was surprised by how relatively thoughtful/mild-mannered he seemed to other comedians I've heard on the show. And, I found out he wrote a book, so I had to read it. I've never read a book or writings from a comedian, so I thought it'd be entertaining, and it was.

Not lifechanging. Not jaw-dropping. Not awe inspiring. But definitely entertaining, and honestly that's good enough when it comes to books in my opinion. I think I would've liked for the book to delve more into his upbringing, outlook on life, and particular approach/style to comedy (and there is a little bit of that), but it's more often about funny encounters he's had on airplane flights with celebrities. Also, some of the stories he wrote about are stories he has told for comedy specials and the sort, so if you're any bit familiar with any of his comedic work, then the book can feel a bit repetitious at times. Regardless, it was easy to read and something you could clear through in like 2-3 day if you just want to read funny stories about an up-and-coming comic who found his way into success, and all the wacky memories that accompany such a journey.

The quote above is him describing his 3 year old son Julian who seems to have an identity crisis. It starts from an early age, I see...

The Head Hurts by the Heart Knows the Truth by Headache

You know how some songs close with audio recordings from the artist playing over the track? This album is like that. Except it's every song and all the time.

But I like it. It reminds me of a handful of electronic songs I enjoy that typically include some random audio sample of Alan Watt's saying some philosophical shit about us being the universe experiencing itself or whatever. Similar to UK drill, you either appreciate this type of music for the art form that it is, or you're just wrong and should reevaluate your taste.

Also, I like this album from a practical perspective as well. It's really easy to listen to. I struggle with listening to lyrics in most songs since it's difficult for me to discern the lyrics and their meaning from the melody, especially when they're so intertwined and it's easy to get lost in the rhythm and key changes (e.g. Pumped Up Kicks by Foster the People). But this album is different. It's not fair to say that lyrics are not the focus of other albums, but I do think this album presents a particular unique emphasis on the spoken word component to songwriting, and it's fun for me to engage that part of my brain when listening to this album.

If you're interested in listening to some of the songs, I'd recommend the following:

Business Opportunities
The Beginning of The End

Tiktok

So, this blog came out a bit later than I would've liked, but I'm trying to be okay with letting life happen slower than I'd like it to. These past couple of months, I've been particularly antsy to meet arbitrary deadlines surrounding my studying, blogging, & reading, and I'm coming to realize that I need to enjoy the process more. I often think about how when people say they've achieved their goals, it's much more unsatisfying than they've anticipated. And that makes me nervous. I consider myself a hard worker, but without getting existential, what's the point of my hard work if I struggle to enjoy the present moment. I'm always thinking "what's next?", and I always feel like what's next has to be extraordinary.

So, I'm trying to slow things down. I tried to optimize days, and now I'm optimizing weeks. 'Optimization' still seems like a flawed way to approach how one lives their life, but it's an improvement nonetheless, and progress happens SLOWLY, right? It hasn't been a particularly lifechanging experience thus far, but it has been a lot better. Thinking in the span of weeks has led me to feel less guilty if a day "flops", and I don't make the most of it like I would've liked; that used to give me serious stress, and now... less stress. Has it reached eustress? Not quite, but we're getting there.

What's inspired this change? Well, I feel like I've realized the benefits of waiting some things out and being patient. For example:

I lost my work laptop on an American Airlines flight 2 months ago. They just emailed me last week that they found it.

I've been growing out my hair, and I like how it's come along. Some days I was tempted to cut it and go back to my old hairstyle, but not anymore.

I've saved pretty aggressively since graduating college. Sure, I can't buy a house, but I can comfortably afford any trips, meals, & clothes that I'd like. That's something I shouldn't take for granted.

I don't care much for shopping, but I've realized how important it is to wear clothes you feel comfortable in, so I'm willing to dedicate time towards it.

I started climbing about 10 months ago, and it's nice to build a skill and bring my friends out to try a hobby that I enjoy.

I started coding a year ago. I went the past 4 years thinking it might be too hard for me, but I've challenged myself and discovered otherwise.

And probably a variety of either things where I've realized that it's important to invest time, be patient, and let it pay off in due time. Speaking of being patient, if anyone wants to try Pollo Campero with me LEMME KNOW. I first saw them in Guatemala, but I never had the chance to try it then. Come to find out, this chicken chain is also in NYC. Does it compete with Cain's? Guatemalans might agree, so I'd like to find out myself.
